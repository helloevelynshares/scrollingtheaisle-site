Founder story

I started Scrolling the Aisle because I genuinely love grocery stores.

Not just for the deals, although I absolutely love a good deal, but for the fun of wandering around and seeing what’s new. New snacks, seasonal finds, local price drops, limited-time flavors, and random products you didn’t know existed until they showed up on the shelf. There is so much innovation happening in the grocery aisles, and I love it.

Whenever something catches my eye, my first instinct is to take a picture and send it to my sister.

Then the same questions always come up: is this new, have you tried it, and how good is it?

Time disappears in a grocery store, and Scrolling the Aisle is my attempt to turn that pastime into something useful, and a little more communal.

The site helps people discover new grocery finds, follow local store changes, compare prices, and spot when something is actually a good deal. Sometimes that means berries or Cheetos are cheaper than usual. Sometimes it means a new snack just showed up. Sometimes it means starting an online debate over whether something is worth the hype.

It’s part deal tracker, part grocery discovery feed, and part place for people who love the aisles to figure out together: how good is this?
